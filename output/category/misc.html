About Me

I'm a PhD candidate at UNSW Canberra, graduating in March 2025. To the right, you can see me and Cheomseongdae (첨성대 is the oldest surviving astronomical observatory in Asia, located in Gyeongju/South Korea) in the background.

I started my BSc in Astronomy on 2016 at Pontificia Universidad Catolica de Chile (PUC) and graduated around mid 2020 by presenting my thesis titled Period-change rates in Large Magellanic Cloud Cepheids revisited with Prof. Marcio Catelan as my advisor. Then, in 2021 I joined UNSW Canberra for a PhD in Physics under the supervision of Dr. Ashley Ruiter, with whom I am working on binary systems involving both white dwarf and sub-dwarf B stars by using the rapid binary population synthesis code COMPAS. My PhD thesis is related to the formation, evolution and characteristics of sub-dwarf B stars.

I'm generally interested in Stellar Astrophysics, whether it is stellar structure and evolution, variability, modelling or any star-related things you might think-of. Other than that, I also have additional experience in different topics. This includes a short summer-research at PUC under Prof. Franz Bauer (AGN-related) and a summer internship at ESO Chile under Dr. Claudia Paladini (Interferometry by using the MATISSE instrument). You can find more details in my CV.

On my free time I play videogames casually (Mass Effect is one of the reasons why I got into astronomy), read webcomics and practice/learn the Korean language (제 웹사이트 방문하셔서 감사합니다 ㅎㅎ).
